Cave Diver

Procedural Audio Madness





An exploration into procedural audio implementation using Unity and Wwise
It's only natural that a procedurally generated game has procedurally generated audio...

Or at least, that was what I decided on when Cave Diver was first proposed.



The goal of the project was not necessarily to produce a complete, polished experience, but rather to build something that showcases the advanced topic you chose to research. I didn't have much experience in Unity, however I was confident enough about my coding prowess and understanding of audio to take the leap and become the team's sound designer and programmer.

When discussing our desired research topics, a few ideas bounced around. Mark wanted to work on water shaders, Arthur had been experimenting with 3D procedurally-generated spaces, and I had become caught up in a few GDC talks about procedural music and sound.

Have all the audio be procedurally generated.
